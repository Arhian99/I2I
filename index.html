My name is Arhian Albis Ramos. I am a self-taught web developer based in Austin, Texas. In my current role as an automation lab associate at Rules-Based Medicine (RBM) I assist in the development and maintenance of scripts that control our automated liquid handling robot platforms.

As a self-taught web developer, I think it is important I touch on how I got into programming. My traditional educational background is in Biochemistry. I earned a Bachelor of Science in Biochemistry from The University of Texas at Austin in May 2021, after which I went on to pursue a Doctor of Medicine (MD) degree at Dell Medical School. My decision to study Biochemistry and subsequently medicine follows a common thread: my passion for critical thinking and my love for the process involved in solving a problem.

During my first year in medical school, I began realizing that while medicine does involve problem solving and critical thinking, it also relies heavily on oneâ€™s ability to memorize and retrieve large amounts of information. While I find this latter skill to be incredibly important and useful, it is not what I find intellectually stimulating. Therefore, throughout my first year of medical school, I taught myself the basics of frontend web development, namely HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The more I learned, the more I fell in love with programming, specifically the use of logic and critical thinking to define a set of end goals. And most importantly, figuring out how to implement a solution that will achieve those end goals in the most efficient and reliable way possible.

After successfully completing my first year of medical school, I made the incredibly difficult decision to stop pursuing medicine so that I can dedicate my full time to learning software development and pursue a full-time software engineer position.

I am currently open to new opportunities including but not limited to mentorship, SWE apprenticeships or internships, freelance projects, and part-time or full-time positions.
